OUR CLAIMS PROCESS

Home > Claims > Process

We all dread it, but sometimes the inevitable happens and we have to seek a claim from our insurer.

What next? Who do we reach out to first? What are your obligations? Such situations can be stressful but with our help, much of it eases out.

24/7 Emergency Claims Support

Contact us immediately after an accident and we shall walk you through the crucial steps that will lead to processing your claim.

+254 775 444 777

Alternatively you can submit the claims yourself by following these 3 easy steps.

1

Step 1 : Claims checklist Complete the relevant checklist as per your cover

MOTOR

Date of Loss (DOL)
  • Claim form duly filled, signed and (stamped for companies)
  • Police abstract
  • Driver's statement
  • Driver's driving licence copy
  • Driver's PIN copy
  • Driver's ID copy
  • Sketch map of scene of accident
  • Company's PIN copy
  • Logbook copy
  • Inspection report (if there are injuries)

FIRE

Date of Loss (DOL)
  • Claim form duly filled, signed and (stamped for companies)
  • Police abstract
  • Owner's (Management) statement
  • Owner's (Company's) PIN copy
  • Valuation report or receipts (if any)

BURGLARY

Date of Loss (DOL)
  • Claim form duly filled, signed and (stamped for companies)
  • Police abstract
  • Owner's (Management) statement
  • Owner's (Company's) PIN copy
  • Invoice or receipts (if any)

WIBA

Date of Loss (DOL)
  • Directorate of Occupational Safety & Health (DOSH) Form 1-4
  • Claim form duly filled, signed and (stamped for companies)
  • Statement of the injured
  • Supervisor's/witness statement
  • Pay slip (of stated period)
  • Sick off sheet
  • Original medical receipts
  • Medical certificate

Frequently Asked Questions

What is covered under motor insurance?
Motor insurance typically covers loss or damage to your vehicle due to accidents, theft, fire, natural disasters, and third-party liability for injury or property damage.
How is the premium calculated?
Premiums are based on factors including the vehicle's make and model, age, engine capacity, your driving history, location, and the coverage options you select.
What is a no-claim bonus?
A no-claim bonus is a discount on your premium for every year you don't make a claim. It can accumulate up to 50% or more over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is covered under home insurance?
Home insurance typically covers the building structure and/or contents against risks like fire, theft, storm damage, and certain natural disasters.
Do I need building or contents cover?
If you own your home, you need building cover. Contents cover is recommended for everyone to protect personal belongings. Renters typically need only contents cover.
